How Sovereign Signal Works

Sovereign Signal is a daily intelligence product that tracks how the United Kingdom is perceived externally across five strategic pillars: soft power, defence credibility, economic leadership, diplomatic influence, and institutional trust.

Unlike survey-based reputation products that rely on predetermined questions and periodic polling, Sovereign Signal processes the live global information environment — the same signals that governments, markets, and institutions respond to every day. It detects what is being said, measures how sentiment is shifting, monitors which narratives are gaining traction, and generates forward-looking predictions about where perception is heading.

The system is built on NoahWire, a narrative signal processing engine designed for the complexity of news-grade information — where tone is mixed, claims compete, and diplomatic language masks shifting positions.

01

Global Ingestion at Scale

Continuously ingests open-source public information across news, wire services, public records, regulatory publications, press releases, corporate disclosures, digital media, broadcast transcripts, social media, blogs, specialist publications, and academic releases. The system processes billions of data packets from sources spanning every major geography and language.

02

Evidence Selection: 24-Hour Corpus

Applies multi-stage filtering to reduce the global stream to approximately 2,000 unique, directly relevant items per 24-hour cycle. Uses deduplication, relevance gating, source normalisation, cross-source reinforcement, and time discipline to ensure every item in the corpus earns its place through evidential weight, not volume.

03

Narrative Signal Processing

Converts evidence into machine-readable narrative structure. Each item is processed for polarity and stance (favourable, adverse, neutral, contested), momentum (rising, fading, stable), discourse temperature, propagation patterns across geographies, authority weighting based on source credibility, and coherence — whether signals reinforce each other or fragment.

04

Why Sentiment Is Different

Designed for news-grade complexity, not product reviews. The system handles mixed tone, indirect criticism, diplomatic language, narrative framing, competing claims, and policy-driven sentiment. It detects institutional caution — the gap between what is said officially and what the evidence suggests — through structured analysis rather than keyword matching.

05

No Predetermined Answers

The system identifies emerging concerns, shifting perceptions, new threats, and contested topics as they are shaped by the evidence itself. There are no predetermined questionnaires or fixed assumptions. Each daily cycle begins with the information environment as it stands, not as it was expected to be.

06

From Measurement to Forecast

Models momentum, heat, authority mix, propagation, coherence, and trigger conditions to estimate where narratives are heading. The system forecasts escalation, persistence, fading, fragmentation, and regional migration — giving decision-makers forward visibility on perception shifts before they become established positions.

07

Built for Scrutiny

Every assessment is time-bounded, built on deduplicated evidence, attributed to identifiable narratives, and accompanied by confidence levels that reflect evidence strength rather than opinion. The system is designed to withstand ministerial-level challenge: every number can be traced back to the signals that produced it.
