Executive Summary

What You Need to Know

APAC financial services institutions face converging pressure from APRA CPS 234 expectations, CrowdStrike’s sub-30-minute breakout times, Microsoft 365 data loss prevention expansion, and cross-border expansion creating control sprawl. RBI reviewed Yes Bank after a BookMyForex card data leak; Cloudflare documented a February 20, 2026 outage from routing changes. Each event tightens the buyer’s calculation on what counts as defensible access control.

Cisco Secure Access aligns where buyers want fewer moving parts and more defensible outputs. Public scope includes Zero Trust Network Access, Secure Web Gateway, and Cloud Access Security Broker integration — matching procurement demand for consistent policy enforcement. But the 50% alignment score reflects a proof gap: public sources confirmed category presence against all five buyer requirements, but none showed independently verifiable APAC finance-specific audit mapping, measured containment outcomes, or multi-country performance benchmarks.

Bottom line: The market clearly needs what Cisco describes. But buyer trust in APAC financial services is earned through publishable evidence packs, in-region pilot outcomes, and audit artefacts mapped to MAS, APRA, HKMA, and RBI expectations — not product scope alone. The constraint is proof threshold rather than category fit.
