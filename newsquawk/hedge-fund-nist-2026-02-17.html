03 Methodology

The Information Asymmetry framework identifies opportunities where material information has not yet propagated through market pricing mechanisms. By analysing the gap between private signal strength and public market consensus, the system quantifies tradeable edges before they decay.

Each opportunity is scored across multiple dimensions: signal freshness (how recently the information entered the system), propagation velocity (how quickly the signal is spreading to mainstream channels), edge quality (the structural durability of the information advantage), and conviction (the statistical confidence in the directional call).

Signals are classified as TRADE (actionable with immediate entry), WATCH (directionally resolved but awaiting catalyst), INVESTIGATE (requires further due diligence), or AVOID (edge has decayed or thesis has been invalidated). Market regime overlays adjust position sizing and risk parameters based on broader macro conditions.
