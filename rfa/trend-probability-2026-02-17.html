Section VIII

Defender Priorities — 90-Day Horizon

Six critical priorities emerge for the coming 90 days. First, RMM governance is now a primary control surface — ransomware operators are leaning on legitimate admin tooling to stay inside normal IT noise. Second, the state-crime overlap is structural, driven by shared chokepoints at identity and edge rather than direct collaboration. Third, patch velocity acceleration continues, with defenders needing to assume active exploitation within 48-72 hours of disclosure.

Fourth, management-plane security is under direct attack — M365 tenant compromise and admin tooling weaknesses remain frequent ransomware entry points. Fifth, plugin and extension ecosystems are operationalising trust-boundary failures, with near-term response centring on connector inventory and disablement. Sixth, attribution ambiguity remains a binding constraint, capping state-ransomware linkage predictions at 50% and requiring defenders to plan for dual-use scenarios regardless of operator labelling.

Momentum Index
Independent velocity measure tracking trend acceleration (start → mid → current). Classifications: Strong (accelerating), Building (growing), Weak (decelerating), Forming (emerging signal).
